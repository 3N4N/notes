## Chapter 1

The wall of Annares port is less a functional boundary and more a metaphorical
one. "Where it crossed the roadway, instead of having a gate it degenerated
into mere geometry, a line, an idea of boundary.

"It enclosed the universe, leaving Annares outside, free." It's ironic because
looked from outside it's Annares that is enclosed, outcast, imprisoned.

The wall is the only boundary wall on Annares. "Nowhere else could they see a
sign that said No Trespassing." That means, everything on Annares was
accessible to all. It's only the other worlds, foreigners, who were to be
feared.

Spectators were excited to see freighters at Annares port. Although they are
apprehensive of the Urrasti, they are curious nonetheless. Some human nature
cannot be denied by social structure.

The foreman of Annares Defense Crew is a woman.

"Bastard" is untranslatable in Pravic. Probably because they don't consider
this concept derogatory.

"No mob of Oddies," says the ship captain of Urras. Oddies is probably a play
on the word, Odonians.

The foreman of the Defence Crew is unarmed. It's unclear at this point if it's
because she is a she or Annaresti don't use firearms in general. A few lines
later, it's mentioned that the mob gathered outside the port don't have
firearms, but it's unclear if firearms are prohibited by law or just
nonextant.

"If the foreman had no experience in bossing a mob, they had no experience in
being one. Members of a community, not elements of a collectivity, they were
not moved by mass feeling; there were as many emotions there as there were
people. And they did not expect commands to be arbitrary, so they had no
practice in disobeying them." [NC]

The mob throw rocks at Shevek. One rock kills a defence crew (Annaresti), but
none of the crew except the foreman is distraught. What's that about?

The mob charges into the ship, unobstructed, and being shocked by alien tech,
they retreat. Clearly, the tech of Annares is lagging behind.

"Autism of terror" - that's what UKLG uses to describe Shevek's experience of
the spaceship lifting off. Is the use of "autism" insensitive here?

"He had always feared that this would happen, more than he had ever feared
death. To die is to lose the self and rejoin the rest. He had kept himself,
and lost the rest." Shevek's thoughts at watching Annares on the viewscreen of
the Mindful, growing distant and smaller.

The polite form of direct address in Iotic was in third person.

"hevek perceived that the little man was upset. He felt no compunction, no
sympathy; but even where he was, in absolute solitude, the one law held, the
one law he had ever acknowledged." What is this one law? In chapter five,
Shevek says, "There is no other reward, on Anarres, no other law. One’s own
pleasure, and the respect of one’s fellows. That is all."

His small room in the Mindful repelled Shevek. It reminded him of a place he
didn't wanna remember.

"There was no time. He was the time; he only. He was the river, the arrow, the
stone." Shevek's thoughts when groggy under vaccines/medicines. The stone is
probably the rock thrown by the mob. But what's the river, the arrow? River
and arrow are both unidirectional. Is that significant? [NC]

Shitstool, not toilet/bathroom, is what Shevek names the excremental facility.
Shows that Annaresti are more comfortable with their bodily functions.

Shevek unwittingly locks himself in the bathroom and gets enraged. Why?

Sleeping clothes is a concept Shevek is unfamiliar with.

"Not that you [Shevek] is an alien in the same sense," Kimoe says.

"It costs less," Kimoe says as explanation of burning cheap clothes instead of
cleaning them. It's a capitalistic explanation.

"The creation of pseudo-species" - looking on all foreigners as inferior.

"Interstellar travel is so *costly* and so slow," Kimoe says.

Urrasti often used "higher" as a synonym for "better" (because their structure
is pyramidal); Annaresti would use "more central."

"Any *enlightened* person..." Kimoe differentiates people who regard
established religions as proper religion from the enlightened ones. What does
enlightened mean to a Urrasti? Is it the same as Earth's enlightenment
philosophy?

"Educated men would understand that, these officers are ignorant," Kimoe says.
It's ironic that he says educated men would not make pseudo-species of
Annaresti because of their lack of established religions, but he explains it
by making pseudo-species of less-educated people.

"Shevek sat in the cushioned, comfotable chair . . . all of it seemed as alien
as it had the first time he saw it." This is how Shevek feels after realizing
the cultural difference between him and Kimoe, of how they view women. UKLG
describes this realization excellently by using the foreign environment of the
spaceship as a metaphor of the foreign culture of Urrasti.

"Were Urrasti cabinetmakers all celibate?" Shevek found the furniture on the
Mindful overtly erotic, and he is sure that he hadn't been apart from Takver
long enough to see a woman on every tabletop, i.e., it's not his subjective
perception. It's probably because the whole Urrasti crew was male and a
capitalistic marketplace would try to cater to their gender-specific want.

"He [Shevek] was aware of the three men [Urrasti], of their attitudes,
protective, respectful, proprietary." Why *proprietary*? They do feel
responsible for his well-being (protective). Is that the same as proprietary
for an Annaresti?

The Urrasti officials (principal of university, president of senate) welcoming
Shevek wore extravagant dresses. He used the word "splendor." Is ceremonial
clothing related to pyramidal social structure? A way to differentiate your
status perhaps.


## Chapter 2

The chapter starts in present tense, in a omniscient pov. But in the middle of
a conversation, it shifts into past. Why is that?

It's a memory of a toddler named Shev, probably Shevek, who has a
confrontation with another toddler about a patch of sunlight. He learns that
no one owns anything, they share. The scene shows the innate human nature to
lay possessive authority over things.

A kid Shevek explains Zeno's paradox to his group. Only he doesn't know it by
that name. He invented it himself. But the director doesn't believe him, and
shuts him up. He says, "Speech is sharing, a cooperative art. You’re not
sharing, merely egoizing." What does it tell us about Annaresti society?

The group mentioned above was sitting in a circle. After Shevek is discharged,
they start a group story, taking turns. So maybe Shevek's attitude of a
professor, a hierarchically superior person, was what annoyed the director.

Shevek pontificates on how numbers are less ambiguous than words.

Shevek also thinks about a Square, which must be some arrangement of numbers
with special properties. Unclear.

Palat got a job at a water reclamation factor, which means Annares has low
supply of water.

Palat tells Shevek about the woman he (P) will have sex with. It again
implies, like the use of the word "shitstool," that Annaresti are comfortable
with their bodily needs and functions.

Most books on Annares is "bound in green with the *Circle of Life* stamped on
the cover."

"Into his hands he received the covenant of eternal justice." That's how UKLG
describes Shevek handling a book on log tables.

Shevek dreams of a wall like the one around Annares port. It bars him from
going home. His father, possibly his mother too, tells him to look at a stone.
In it Shevek finds first the number 5 (plurality) and then 1 (unity). "That is
the cornerstone," says *a voice of dear familiarity*, whatever that means.
Shevek remembers the ecstatic feeling of this realization but cannot attain it
later, either in the waking world or that of dreaming.

Shevek and several other boys are excited by the foreign idea of prison and
forced labour. They playact a prison scenario, selecting Kadagv from a crowd
of volunteers to be detained for two days. Although they start it as a play,
after seeing what it does to Kadagv, they are horrified. Shevek for the first
time experiences guilt of doing a bad deed and the fear of being found out.

The boys exclude girls from their hangout sessions. Interesting because in
Annaresti in most cases gender is inconsequential. Does the boys' need to
segregate themselves show that it's an inherent urge?

"Where then is Truth?" "In the hill one happens to be sitting on." Reminiscent
of the wall metaphor: "What was inside it and what was outside it depended
upon which side of it you were on."

In a film about Odonian Movement, it's shown the Urrasti babies have hair like
the Annaresti people. Does that mean Urrasti adults shave or is there some
genetic mutation on Urras?

Shevek and his friends discuss why they are given only those info about Urras
that disgusts them, makes them hateful. Tirin argues they should visit Urras
and see the truth for themselves. Shevek is disturbed by the idea and launches
into a pendantic argument that frankly doesn't make much sense. [NC]

Shevek was "called upon" for the afforestation project. But don't the
Annaresti believe in volunteer work?

The muscular physique of Gimar, a workmate of Shevek, awes him at first, but
then he becomes "strong enough to desire her." Does *strong* here mean
physical strength or mental maturity? Either way, it displays Annaresti men,
at least young ones, have standards for female beauty similar to ours.

After rejection from a potential mate Shevek perceives the weather as too hot.
But after copulating with a woman, his first real sex, he perceives the same
weather as hot not in a uncomfortable way but in an welcome manner. "And the
days were long, and hot, and bright, and the dust smelled like Beshun’s body."

Shevek is irritated by having to work in a plantation project, which is not
related to his department, Physics. But when he sees what the project results
in, a mist of green on the desert, he is tearful.

"What a man wants is freedom. What a woman wants is property," Vokep says,
which shoves Shevek into a train of thought about possession in sex. [NC]

"To go was not enough for him [Shevek], only half enough; he must come back."

"The very nature of the voyage, like a circumnavigation of the globe, implied
return." [NC]

"You can go home again, the General Temporal Theory asserts, so long as you
understand that home is a place where you have never been." [NC]

"Some emperors actually have new clothes."

Shevek (19) loves Mitis (55). Is this love sexual or general?

Shevek et al have a discussion about suffering and happiness in the context of
Odonian philosophy.

## Chapter 3

Shevek uses the word "shit" in its functional sense, with no sense of vulgar.
But in ch 2 the girl that rejected Shevek's advances used "Shit" as a curse.
Then again, there was no exclamation mark, so maybe she used it merely as a
conjunction.

"Atro had precisely the one kind of authority that Shevek recognized."
Probably Atro's self-worth at least concerning Physics.

Shevek says, "I come, like a good Odonian, 'with empty hands.'"

Chifoilisk seems anti-government. Good for him.

Shevek indulges in aggrandizing his foray into new types of physics. He
recollects how no one in Urrasti was equal to him with whome he might have
bounced ideas off. UKLG's writing here is blatantly cheesy. Unforgivable for a
writer of her caliber.

When Shevek asks where the women are, the Urrasti scientists interpret as him
asking for prostitutes. Jumping into this conclusion is telling, and
disgusting.

"Shevek returned to sit on the marble seat by the hearth, which he already
felt as his seat, his territory. He wanted a territory." Shevek is showing his
urge to possess, like when as a child he wanted the square patch of sunlight.

When asked if he comes as an emissary of Annaresti, Shevek displays caution.
He acknowledges his own intention, of building walls, and says it's the only
intiative he acknowledges. That means he is not a rep of Annaresti.

Federative vs hierarchical

"The lure and compulsion of profit was evidently a much more effective
replacement of the natural initiative than he had been led to believe."

"Despite the ovation, Shevek had the curious feeling that nobody had heard
it." Shevek's thought of his speech for free communication.

In the first days of his visit, while he was stuck in his room because of
infection, Shevek feels he belongs in the beautiful and "humane" world of
Urras. But after he roams and sees how the world works, he feels he has "no
right to all the grace and bounty of this world."

"The explorer who will not come back or send back his ships to tell his tale
is not an explorer, only an adventurer, and his sons are born in exile."

"He was not part of it. Nor was he part of it. Nor was he part of the world of
his birth." What's the significance of this repetition? Can't be merely to
emphasize. Because the structure is not simple repetition. She didn't write,
"No, he was not part of it," neither did she write, "And it was not part of
him." These three sentences are strikingly unusual.

"He was alone, here, because he came from a self-exiled society. He had always
been alone on his own world because he had exiled himself from his society.
... And he had been fool enough to think that he might serve to bring together
two worlds to which he did not belong."

"The light of his world filled his empty hands." Shevek associated his empty
hands as an Odonian attribute. Is the light of Annares a symbol for Annaresti
culture, which is what Shevek brings for the Urrasti, not an empty hand as he
claimed.

## Chapter 4

Starts with an info dump about Annaresti settlement, but it's framed as if
Shevek, while in a dirigible toward Abbenay, is going over the history in his
mind. That doesn't make it less boring, but an interesting hack.

Abbenay was close to equator, and most buildings are low, so there is no
shadow. "There was a vividness to things, a hardness of edge and corner, a
clarity. Everything stood out separate, itself." I don't know if it's a symbol
of something.

In a later para, Odo's philosophy is cited: "Excess is excrement," and UKLG
says, "Abbenay was poisonless . . . Nothing was hidden." It explains the
previous point of Abbenay's shadowless city being a symbol of some kind.

While under the shade of leafy trees, as opposed to spiky holum trees, Shevek
wonders if the leaves aren't excess/excrement. He also cannot help but feel
blessed by the green hands protecting him from above.

Sabul treats simultaneity like it's mysticism. He also asks Shevek not to
share the books he is given to read, likening it to sharing bombs with
children. Probably shows Physics syndic as a rigid cult?

A exposition on Shevek's introversion. Boring and repetitive.

"The most brilliant insights of Sabul’s own works on Sequency were in fact
translations from the Iotic, unacknowledged." LOL.

So not only was Shevek not to share his Urrasti physics books, he was not to
admit he learned Iotic or that it can be learned. Is it so that the physics
syndic can tout the Urrasti physics as their own, like Sabul does? Or is it
general of all the syndics, so the people are not exposed to Urrasti knowledge
of their own choice.

Shevek wonders why Center of Sciences had desserts every night whereas in
other communities they had it once/twice a week. He also wondered why he was
given a single room, whereas everywhere else everyone lived in dorms. Feeling
guilty he stops taking extra desserts, but he didn't move into a dorm and kept
his single room, because it helped his work and his work was important.

Shevek falls ill and is admitted to a hospital where Rulag visits him. She
tries to reconnect but Shevek denies, even though he feels an urge to
reconnect. It's unclear if Shevek denies her because of anger or a sense of
betrayal of Palat.

## Chapter 5

Shevek is appalled by the exam system, and reading his reaction is probably
the greatest pleasure I had recently.

To blend in, Shevek buys Urrasti clothing. Section break. Some time passes.
And UKLG employs an interesting technique. First, Shevek adapts more into
Urrasti culture, but that's not the technique. In a dinner scene, UKLG writes,
"He greeted someone at another table and moved on." *Someone* is a striking
word of choice. Since we are watching Shevek after a period of transition,
this word tells us that Shevek has made some acquaintances, which means that
he has adapted to the new culture and environment. And it's cinematic, too.
This is some staggering level of competent writing.

Chifoilisk comes out as a nationalist socialist. Shevek and he have a debate
about how Ioti govt is using Shevek for GTT and if Thu isn't more suitable for
a partnership with Annares.

Chif is called back to Thu. Why? Because he alerted Shevek of his importance?
told him to be cautious? Ooh. An air of mystery.

## Chapter 7

Shevek gets close with Oiie's family, specially with the children, and one night
he meets Oiie's sister, Vea.

Shevek and Vea have instant connection, seems romantic from Vea's side, but it
also seems she might be trying to draw Shev in, that is, she may be an agent.

Shevek is frustrated with being ineffective, in his work of physics and in
breaking down the wall, and rebelliously skips town and visits Nio Esseia and
meets up with Vea.

In Nio Esseia, Shev is surprised by the politeness of the shopkeepers and
wonders if it's the money that makes them so. A line of thought I remember from
the sexworker character in A Bride for Rip Van Winkle. Although she said that we
use money to undervalue our politeness because otherwise we would be overwhelmed
by the positivity.

When asked why women let men keep them down, Vea answers it's easier. The men do
all the hard work and the women control the men. Plus women have all the
luxuries. Vea also says any woman from Annares would choose Urrasti lifestyle.

Shev and Vea discuss the value of morality.

Under the influence of alcohol, which is new to him, Shevek gets excited by Vea
and tries to rape her. She was intentionally alluring him and she was inclined
to have sex with Shev but not at that time. Whether it's only because of alcohol
or because he is unfamiliar to the culture, Shev doesn't understand why Vea
would be in a barechested dress, flirting with him, kissing him, if she didn't
want to have sex. It's a messy situation. Hard to disentagle Shev's psychology.

## Chapter 8

Famine in Annares. Everyone has to work a lot. Solidarity is tested.

Shev is on post while Takver takes care of the baby. When Shev comes back,
Takver is on post with the baby and Shev cannot join them because there is no
job posting open and he is needed somewhere else.

Shev thinks about how Odo foresaw this strong moral obligation arising from
work, for which reason she made in the Pravic language work and play the same
word. It should let people be content with working. Presented as a good thing,
it is exactly how a authoritarian society would implement things. Newspeak,
anyone? Here we see the wall metaphor again. What is imprisoned depends on which
side you are on.

"The demonstration is going to be absolutely *nonviolent*," Maedda said with
